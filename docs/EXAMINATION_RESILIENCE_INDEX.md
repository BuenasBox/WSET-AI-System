# EpistemicLab Examination Resilience Index

**Purpose:** Deterministic concept-coverage resilience only.  
**Not measured:** marks, pass probability, merit, distinction, readiness claims,
or examination outcomes.

## 1. Index definition

Each subtopic receives six binary evidence dimensions:

1. Concept coverage: at least three SBA or equivalent structured items.
2. Causal reasoning: at least one causal chain.
3. Written articulation: at least one confidently mapped OR item.
4. Misconception coverage: at least one operational misconception node.
5. SAT reasoning: relevant SAT practice where applicable.
6. Coaching coverage: at least one explicit coaching/remediation path.

Classification:

- **HIGH:** five or six dimensions, with concept, causal, and written evidence.
- **MEDIUM:** three or four dimensions, including concept evidence.
- **LOW:** one or two dimensions, or severe imbalance within a covered topic.
- **MISSING:** no defensible evidence.

SAT is applicable to tasting rows. For theory rows, absence of SAT does not
reduce the classification.

## 4. Interpretation

The corpus is resilient where multiple evidence types converge, not merely
where raw item volume is high. RA1 therefore scores highly overall despite its
hard Icewine gap. RA2 scores only medium despite 217 SBA because its official
weight is 56% and several regions lack causal and written repetition. RA3 and
RA4 are the main examination-resilience priorities.

The 20/20 misconception closure improves operational resilience but does not
change a subtopic to HIGH unless a relevant misconception node actually exists.
