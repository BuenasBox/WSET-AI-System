## NEW MISCONCEPTIONS BY DOMAIN (23 TOTAL)

### Icewine & Cryoconcentration (2)
1. **MC_ICEWINE_NOBLE_01**: "All Icewine requires noble rot (Botrytis)"  
   - **Corrected:** Icewine is produced by freezing grapes; noble rot is NOT required
   - **Detection signals:** "Icewine needs botrytis to be sweet"
   - **Severity:** Medium | **Distinction relevance:** Yes

2. **MC_ICEWINE_RESIDUAL_02**: "All Icewines have very high residual sugar (200+ g/L)"  
   - **Corrected:** Residual sugar in Icewine varies (80-200+ g/L) depending on freeze conditions
   - **Detection signals:** "All Icewines must have over 200 g/L"
   - **Severity:** Low | **Distinction relevance:** No

### Tokaji Classification & Production (2)
3. **MC_TOKAJI_QUALITY_01**: "Puttonyos rating (1-6) determines final sweetness absolutely"  
   - **Corrected:** Puttonyos indicates Aszú proportion, but final sweetness depends on base wine RS, ageing, vintage
   - **Detection signals:** "Puttonyos number directly tells you the residual sugar"
   - **Severity:** Medium | **Distinction relevance:** Yes

4. **MC_TOKAJI_AGING_02**: "Tokaji must age in oak for 5+ years minimum to be authentic"  
   - **Corrected:** Tokaji ageing requirements vary by type; duration is a winemaking choice
   - **Detection signals:** "All Tokaji must age for 5 years minimum"
   - **Severity:** Low | **Distinction relevance:** No

### Sauternes & Noble Rot Production (2)
5. **MC_SAUTERNES_HARVEST_01**: "Sauternes requires harvest at a fixed Brix level (e.g., 20 Brix)"  
   - **Corrected:** Harvest timing prioritizes botrytis development over a fixed Brix reading; vintage-dependent
   - **Detection signals:** "Sauternes is made from grapes at a specific sugar level"
   - **Severity:** Medium | **Distinction relevance:** Yes

6. **MC_SAUTERNES_SWEETNESS_02**: "Sauternes is always the sweetest dessert wine"  
   - **Corrected:** Icewines, Tokajis, and other styles can exceed Sauternes in residual sugar
   - **Detection signals:** "Sauternes is always the sweetest wine"
   - **Severity:** Low | **Distinction relevance:** No

### Sweet Wine Concentration Methods (2)
7. **MC_SWEET_CONCENTRATION_01**: "Botrytis is the only way to concentrate sweet wines"  
   - **Corrected:** Multiple methods exist: cryoconcentration, drying, late harvest, fortification, other dehydration
   - **Detection signals:** "All sweet wines are made with botrytis"
   - **Severity:** Medium | **Distinction relevance:** Yes

8. **MC_SWEET_BALANCING_02**: "High residual sugar = high quality in sweet wines"  
   - **Corrected:** Quality depends on balance between RS, acidity, alcohol, and complexity
   - **Detection signals:** "High residual sugar means the wine is of excellent quality"
   - **Severity:** Medium | **Distinction relevance:** Yes

### Port Classification & Fortification (2)
9. **MC_PORT_VINTAGE_01**: "All Port wines age 10+ years in barrel"  
   - **Corrected:** Vintage Port ages briefly in barrel (2-3y) then in bottle; Tawny Port ages in barrel (10/20/30/40+ years)
   - **Detection signals:** "All Port wines must age for 10 years in barrel"
   - **Severity:** Medium | **Distinction relevance:** Yes

10. **MC_PORT_FORTIFICATION_02**: "Port is fortified at a random point during fermentation"  
    - **Corrected:** Fortification timing is deliberate; earlier = sweeter, later = drier
    - **Detection signals:** "Port makers add brandy at any point they feel like"
    - **Severity:** Low | **Distinction relevance:** No

### Madeira & Oxidative Ageing (2)
11. **MC_MADEIRA_OXIDATION_01**: "Madeira's oxidized character is a production flaw"  
    - **Corrected:** Oxidation is intentional (Estufagem); characteristic browning is a quality marker
    - **Detection signals:** "Madeira tastes oxidized because it was stored badly"
    - **Severity:** High | **Distinction relevance:** Yes

12. **MC_MADEIRA_SWEETNESS_02**: "All Madeira is sweet"  
    - **Corrected:** Madeira exists in four sweetness levels: Dry, Medium Dry, Medium Sweet, Sweet
    - **Detection signals:** "All Madeira is sweet wine"
    - **Severity:** Low | **Distinction relevance:** No

### Sherry, Flor & Oxidation (2)
13. **MC_SHERRY_FLOR_01**: "Flor develops on all Sherry styles equally"  
    - **Corrected:** Flor develops on Fino/Manzanilla (encouraged); Oloroso deliberately suppresses it
    - **Detection signals:** "All Sherry has flor on the surface"
    - **Severity:** Medium | **Distinction relevance:** Yes

14. **MC_SHERRY_SOLERA_02**: "Solera ageing is unique to Sherry"  
    - **Corrected:** Solera is used primarily for Sherry but has been applied elsewhere (other Spanish regions)
    - **Detection signals:** "Solera aging only happens in Sherry"
    - **Severity:** Low | **Distinction relevance:** No

### Oxidative vs. Biological Ageing (2)
15. **MC_OXIDATIVE_VS_BIOLOGICAL_01**: "Oxidative ageing is always inferior to biological ageing"  
    - **Corrected:** Both methods produce stylistically different wines; neither is inherently superior
    - **Detection signals:** "Oxidative aging is always worse than protected aging"
    - **Severity:** Medium | **Distinction relevance:** Yes

16. **MC_BIOLOGICAL_PROTECTION_02**: "Flor completely prevents all oxidation"  
    - **Corrected:** Flor significantly slows oxidation but does not completely prevent it; some minimal change continues
    - **Detection signals:** "Flor completely stops all oxidation"
    - **Severity:** Low | **Distinction relevance:** No

### Fortification & Production Method Timing (1)
17. **MC_FORTIFICATION_TIMING_01**: "All fortified wines are fortified after fermentation ends"  
    - **Corrected:** Port is fortified during fermentation (mid-fermentation); Sherry after fermentation
    - **Detection signals:** "All fortified wines are fortified after fermentation ends"
    - **Severity:** Medium | **Distinction relevance:** Yes

### Sparkling Wine Production (1)
18. **MC_SPARKLING_TRADITIONAL_01**: "Traditional method must age 10+ years on lees for quality"  
    - **Corrected:** Minimum requirements are 15 months (Champagne) or 9 months (Cava); quality producers exceed minimums
    - **Detection signals:** "All quality Champagne must age for 10 years minimum"
    - **Severity:** Low | **Distinction relevance:** No

### Advanced Viticulture & Quality Factors (5)
19. **MC_CANOPY_VIGOR_EXPOSURE_01**: "High canopy vigor always produces low-quality fruit"  
    - **Corrected:** Moderate vigor with management produces quality; excessive vigor shades fruit; low vigor stresses vines
    - **Detection signals:** "A vigorous canopy always makes bad wine"
    - **Severity:** Medium | **Distinction relevance:** No

20. **MC_YIELD_CONCENTRATION_02**: "Lower yield always produces higher quality"  
    - **Corrected:** Lower yield can enhance concentration, but no absolute relationship; optimal yield varies by terroir/variety
    - **Detection signals:** "The lowest-yielding vineyard always makes the best wine"
    - **Severity:** Low | **Distinction relevance:** No

21. **MC_COLD_SOAK_EXTRACTION_01**: "Cold soaking always extracts more color and tannin"  
    - **Corrected:** Extraction results vary by temperature, duration, pH, grape maturity; not universally necessary
    - **Detection signals:** "Cold soaking always makes wine more colored and tannic"
    - **Severity:** Low | **Distinction relevance:** No

22. **MC_TEMPERATURE_INFLUENCE_01**: "Warmer vintages always produce better wines"  
    - **Corrected:** Warmth is necessary but excessive heat can create unbalanced wines; optimal temperature varies by region/variety
    - **Detection signals:** "Warm vintages are always better than cool vintages"
    - **Severity:** Medium | **Distinction relevance:** Yes

23. **MC_CLIMATE_INFLUENCE_EXTRAS**: Completed with advanced climate/yield/canopy reasoning
