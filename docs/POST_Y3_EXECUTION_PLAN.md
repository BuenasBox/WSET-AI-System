## 2. Sampling principles

- Never delete or deactivate overrepresented content for balance alone.
- Apply quotas before randomisation.
- Use largest-remainder allocation for non-50-item SBA sessions.
- Rotate tied minority-domain slots across sessions.
- Exclude the two unmapped SBA records from RA-proportional sessions.
- Require specification-valid content mapping for OR selection.
- Treat RA1 as foundational in written prompts, even when the prompt's primary
  RA is RA2, RA3, or RA4.
- Keep SAT as one white and one red in Full Simulation.

## 3. Diagnostic SBA blueprints

| Mode | Total | RA1 | RA2 | RA3 | RA4 | RA5 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Quick | 5 | 1 | 3 | 1 rotating slot | 0 rotating slot | 0 rotating slot |
| Express | 10 | 2 | 5 | 1 | 1 | 1 |
| Standard | 25 | 4 | 14 | 3 | 2 | 2 |
| Mock Theory | 50 | 8 | 28 | 5 | 5 | 4 |

For Quick, allocate RA1=1 and RA2=3. The fifth slot rotates RA3, RA4, RA5 in
the sequence RA3 -> RA4 -> RA5, preserving minority-domain exposure over three
sessions without pretending five questions can represent all five weights.
For Standard, rotate the extra 0.5 allocation between RA3 and RA4.

## 4. Adaptive Session blueprints

| Mode | Total | Base quota | Adaptive allowance |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Express | 10 | 2/5/1/1/1 | Replace at most 2 slots within the same RA |
| Standard | 25 | 4/14/3/2/2 | Replace at most 5 slots within the same RA |
| Mock Theory | 50 | 8/28/5/5/4 | No cross-RA replacement |

The order is RA1/RA2/RA3/RA4/RA5. Adaptation should choose topic, difficulty,
misconception, or review items inside each RA quota. It should not allow a
learner weakness to erase official-domain balance.

## 5. Open Response Lab blueprints

The current modes contain 1, 2, 4, and 4 items. Exact official written-paper
weighting cannot be represented in every very short session, so use a rotating
primary-RA schedule while embedding RA1 causal explanation in every prompt.

| Mode | Total | Recommended selection |
|---|---:|---|
| Short | 1 | Rotate RA2, RA3, RA4, RA5; RA2 appears in 7 of each 10 sessions |
| Standard | 2 | 1 RA2 + 1 rotating RA2/RA3/RA4/RA5 |
| Extended | 4 | 3 RA2 + 1 rotating RA3/RA4/RA5 |
| Mock Theory | 4 | 3 RA2 + 1 rotating RA3/RA4/RA5 |

Three-mock rotation:

| Mock | Q1 | Q2 | Q3 | Q4 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| A | RA2 | RA2 | RA2 | RA3 |
| B | RA2 | RA2 | RA2 | RA4 |
| C | RA2 | RA2 | RA2 | RA5 |

Across the cycle this yields 75% RA2 prompts and one prompt from each smaller
written domain. All prompts should require relevant RA1 factor application.
