# EpistemicLab Post-Y3 Content Roadmap

**Audit date:** 2026-06-15  
**Status:** Evidence-based planning only. No implementation or content creation is authorized by this document.

## Priority 1 - Highest pedagogical ROI

### 1. Close hard missing knowledge cells

Create the smallest complete bundles for:

- Icewine/Eiswein
- VDN/Rutherglen/Marsala
- Cava/Cremant causal reasoning
- Prosecco/Sekt causal reasoning

Each bundle should eventually contain SBA, causal chain, micro-drill, OR, misconception, and coaching support. Evidence: these cells are missing or weak across nearly every audited modality.

### 2. Correct Open Response domain imbalance

First OR additions should cover RA3, RA4, and RA5, which have zero committed deployed items. Add RA2 regional application before adding more standalone RA1 climate/viticulture prompts.

Evidence:

- Current OR baseline: RA1 21, RA2 10, RA3-RA5 0.
- Official short-answer emphasis is RA2-heavy, with RA3/RA4 and RA5 also present.

### 3. Build misconception coverage where none exists

First new misconception families:

1. yield
2. canopy
3. irrigation/water stress
4. sparkling-method hierarchy
5. fortification timing
6. Sherry sweetness/flor
7. service/fault/sediment
8. food-pairing absolutes

Evidence: these are high-frequency teaching concepts with no explicit misconception object.

### 4. Rebalance future SBA toward RA2 and RA4

Minimum weighting deficit:

- RA2: 107
- RA4: 27
- RA5: 7

Do not prioritize more generic RA1 climate, fermentation, oak, or tannin recall. Those areas are already overrepresented.

### 5. Expand SAT calibration, not generic SAT volume

Add prompts for readiness edge cases, faults, rose, and closely matched wines with different quality conclusions.

Evidence: all six prompts cover SAT structure, but readiness and nose discrimination remain weak and archetype diversity is narrow.

## Priority 2 - Medium ROI

### 6. Strengthen thin regional reasoning

Ranked regional needs:

1. USA/Canada
2. South Africa
3. Rhone/Southern France
4. Portugal
5. Australia and New Zealand causal depth

These regions have factual SBA coverage but limited chains, OR, misconceptions, or coaching.

### 7. Complete winery-process continuity

Add content for:

- crushing and pressing
- clarification
- stabilisation
- filtration
- bottling/closures
- rose production

Evidence: fermentation, extraction, MLF, lees, and oak are much stronger than the stages before and after them.

### 8. Separate sweet-wine mechanisms

Ensure future content discriminates:

- noble rot
- late harvest
- passito/appassimento
- freezing
- fermentation arrest
- regional examples such as Tokaji and Sauternes

Evidence: botrytis is adequate, passito is weak, and Icewine is missing.

### 9. Add domain-specific coaching pathways

The command-verb and SAT coaching layers are useful but generic. Add content-domain remediation pathways tied to named weaknesses and misconceptions.

## Priority 3 - Nice to have

### 10. Increase within-domain variety after core balance is achieved

Examples:

- additional minor appellations
- second-order law distinctions
- more producer/trade scenarios
- additional SAT wines within already represented archetypes

These should wait because they add breadth where the current problem is missing modality depth.

### 11. Add contrastive content

High-value future pairs:

- traditional versus tank method
- Fino versus Oloroso
- Ruby versus Tawny Port
- botrytis versus passito versus Icewine
- maritime versus continental climate
- new versus used oak
- fault versus normal development

Contrastive objects can cover more than one matrix gap efficiently.

## Stop/go decision

The next content phase should be **GO only for the Priority 1 gaps** after current OR work is reconciled. It should be **NO-GO for generic RA1 expansion**, broad unweighted SBA growth, or additional Sherry-heavy content.

The highest-return sequence is:

1. missing knowledge bundles
2. OR balance
3. misconception families
4. RA2/RA4 SBA balance
5. SAT calibration diversity
