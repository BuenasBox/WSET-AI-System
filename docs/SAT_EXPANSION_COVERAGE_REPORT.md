## Coverage by Learning Objective

### 1. Chardonnay Calibration

#### Cool-Climate (Chablis) — SAT_WINE_001
- **Misconceptions Corrected:**
  - "All Chardonnay is oaky"
  - "Low aroma intensity means low quality"
  - Chablis-Sauvignon Blanc confusion
- **Key Observations:**
  - Pale lemon, high acidity, citrus/green apple, light-medium body
  - Mineral/lees notes from limestone terroir
  - Quality from balance and precision, not richness
- **Causal Reasoning:**
  - Cool climate → high acidity + restrained fruit
  - Minimal oak → neutral profile
  - Kimmeridgian limestone → mineral character
- **Coaching Opportunities:**
  - "Is this wine neutral or precise and balanced?"
  - "How does acidity support quality here?"
  - "Can you identify climate impact from the wine alone?"
- **Exam Value:** Very High

#### Warm Climate with Oak (Burgundy) — SAT_WINE_002
- **Misconceptions Corrected:**
  - "All Burgundy white is the same"
  - Oak-derived aromas confused with primary fruit
  - Full body/butter = sweetness
- **Key Observations:**
  - Medium gold colour, ripe stone fruit, medium to full body
  - Butter, toast, creamy texture from MLF/oak/lees
  - Medium acidity despite richness
- **Causal Reasoning:**
  - Warmer terroir → riper fruit progression
  - Oak/lees contact → secondary aromas (butter, toast)
  - Malolactic conversion → reduced acidity, increased creaminess
- **Coaching Opportunities:**
  - "Separate primary fruit from secondary winemaking notes"
  - "Does oak feel integrated or dominant?"
  - "What tells you this is quality despite richness?"
- **Exam Value:** Very High

**Coverage Result:** Students can distinguish cool-climate restrained style from warm-climate rich style within one grape variety. Builds understanding of terroir and winemaking effect.

### 2. Riesling Calibration

#### Sweet-Acid Balance — SAT_WINE_003 (Mosel)
- **Misconceptions Corrected:**
  - "Sweet wine always means low quality"
  - "Low alcohol means simple wine"
  - "Sweetness masks acidity"
- **Key Observations:**
  - Pale lemon, off-dry to medium-sweet, high acidity, low alcohol (8-9%)
  - Floral and citrus aromas, stone fruit
  - Balance between sweetness and acidity creates quality
- **Causal Reasoning:**
  - Cool climate → high acidity retained, low sugar
  - Harvest timing → residual sugar without overripeness
  - Schist terroir → mineral character
- **Coaching Opportunities:**
  - "Are the sweetness and acidity in balance?"
  - "How does acidity support quality despite residual sugar?"
  - "What indicates ageing potential in sweet wine?"
  - "Notice the low alcohol — what does harvest timing tell you?"
- **Exam Value:** Very High

**Coverage Result:** Students understand that sweetness is a style choice, not a quality marker. High acidity is the key to quality in sweet wine.

### 3. Sauvignon Blanc Calibration

#### Aromatic Intensity — SAT_WINE_004 (Marlborough)
- **Misconceptions Corrected:**
  - "High acidity always equals Riesling"
  - "Green aromas mean underripe"
  - "Intense aromatics are wasted in cheap wine"
  - Confusion between herbaceous (positive) and vegetal (fault)
- **Key Observations:**
  - Pale yellow, dry, high acidity
  - Pronounced intensity: lime, passion fruit, gooseberry, grass
  - Young, varietal-forward profile
- **Causal Reasoning:**
  - Cool/moderate climate → aromatic retention
  - Protective winemaking → volatile preservation
  - Stainless steel + low SO₂ → fresh character
- **Coaching Opportunities:**
  - "Compare aromatic profile: herbaceous/tropical vs floral/petrol?"
  - "How does intensity contribute to quality?"
  - "Distinguish herbaceous (positive) from vegetal (fault)"
  - "What climate and winemaking support this intensity?"
- **Exam Value:** Very High

**Coverage Result:** Students can distinguish Sauvignon Blanc from Riesling by aromatic profile, not just acidity. Value of aromatic intensity is understood.

### 4. Pinot Noir Calibration

#### Pale Colour with High Structure — SAT_WINE_005 (Burgundy)
- **Misconceptions Corrected:**
  - "Pale colour means low quality or weak wine"
  - "High acidity and high tannin always go together"
  - "Earthy/mushroom notes are faults"
  - Colour-structure correlation assumption
- **Key Observations:**
  - Pale ruby to garnet colour despite full structure
  - High acidity, low-medium tannin
  - Red fruit with earthy/tertiary notes
  - Good length and balance
- **Causal Reasoning:**
  - Thin skins → pale colour + lower tannin
  - Cool climate → high acidity + red fruit
  - Bottle age → tertiary development
- **Coaching Opportunities:**
  - "Focus on balance, complexity, length — not colour intensity"
  - "Never infer tannin from colour alone"
  - "Can you identify red fruit under earthy notes?"
  - "What indicates quality in light-coloured red?"
- **Exam Value:** Very High

**Coverage Result:** Students learn critical diagnostic skill: never infer structure from colour. Pinot Noir's contradiction (pale + structured) is pedagogically powerful.

**Coverage Result:** Students understand tannin as separate from acidity, and as the primary structure for ageing in Cabernet Sauvignon.

### 7. Syrah/Shiraz Calibration

#### Climate Expression Variance — SAT_WINE_008
- **Misconceptions Corrected:**
  - "Pepper is always oak-derived"
  - "All Syrah tastes the same (jammy)"
  - "Syrah and Shiraz are different grapes"
- **Key Observations:**
  - Black fruit, white or black pepper, spice
  - Medium+ to full body, medium+ tannin
  - Development varies dramatically by site
- **Causal Reasoning:**
  - Cool site (Rhône) → pepper + freshness
  - Warm site (Barossa) → jammy fruit + higher alcohol
  - Syrah varietal marker → pepper regardless of climate
- **Coaching Opportunities:**
  - "Is fruit fresh black or baked/jammy?"
  - "Identify: white pepper (cool) or black pepper (warm)"
  - "What climate indicators are present?"
  - "How does alcohol reflect terroir?"
- **Exam Value:** High

**Coverage Result:** Students understand Syrah as a climate-sensitive variety and can use pepper character to infer regional expression.

  - "Pale colour + serious structure = diagnostic clue"
  - "Recognize tertiary as positive, not fault"
- **Exam Value:** High

**Coverage Result:** Strongest pedagogical win: Nebbiolo's pale/structured contradiction is the most powerful diagnostic lesson.

### 10. Tempranillo Calibration

#### Oak Ageing & Tertiary Development — SAT_WINE_011 (Rioja Reserva)
- **Misconceptions Corrected:**
  - "Oak spice and vanilla = sweetness"
  - "Oak is always positive without question"
  - "Tertiary is deterioration"
  - Oak dominance and integration confusion
- **Key Observations:**
  - Medium garnet, red/black fruit
  - Vanilla, coconut, spice (oak), leather, tobacco
  - Evolved development from controlled ageing
- **Causal Reasoning:**
  - Rioja Reserva requirement → intentional oak ageing
  - American oak → coconut; French oak → subtle spice
  - Oak + bottle age → spice softening + integration
- **Coaching Opportunities:**
  - "Separate primary, secondary, and tertiary descriptors"
  - "Is oak integrated or does it dominate?"
  - "What indicates quality in an oaked red?"
  - "How does oak ageing support rather than mask?"
- **Exam Value:** Medium-High

**Coverage Result:** Students develop vocabulary for oak-aged wines and understand tertiary complexity as positive.

### 11. Grenache Calibration

#### Alcohol-Body-Tannin Separation — SAT_WINE_012 (Southern Rhône or Spain)
- **Misconceptions Corrected:**
  - "Full body always means high tannin"
  - "Alcohol warmth = sweetness"
  - "Low tannin = low quality"
  - Warm-climate assumptions
- **Key Observations:**
  - Medium ruby, high alcohol, red fruit
  - Low-medium tannin despite full body
  - Soft, plush texture; warm sensation at finish
- **Causal Reasoning:**
  - Warm climate → high sugar → higher alcohol
  - Thin-skinned variety → low tannin extraction
  - Drought tolerance → concentrated fruit + spice
- **Coaching Opportunities:**
  - "Separate body, alcohol level, and tannin assessment"
  - "Notice alcohol warmth on finish — is wine sweet or dry?"
  - "What warm-climate indicators are present?"
  - "How does low acidity affect freshness?"
- **Exam Value:** Medium-High

**Coverage Result:** Students learn to disaggregate body from tannin, and alcohol warmth from sweetness.
