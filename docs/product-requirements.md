# Product Requirements

## Product Goal

Build a professional WSET Level 3 Award in Wines AI learning and evaluation system that helps the primary student prepare for a Distinction-level result by 08 August 2026.

The product must train exam performance, not general wine conversation.

## Non-Goals for This Phase

- No UI
- No frontend
- No backend implementation
- No infrastructure
- No authentication
- No deployment
- No payment system

## Primary Users

- Primary student targeting WSET Level 3 Distinction
- Future students preparing for WSET Level 3
- Tutor or coach reviewing progress and weaknesses

## Core Jobs

- Explain WSET concepts clearly and exam-relevantly.
- Evaluate theory answers with official-like strictness.
- Evaluate SAT tasting notes for structure and consistency.
- Convert knowledge into mark-worthy answers.
- Identify weak areas and prescribe deliberate practice.
- Track progress against exam readiness.

## Product Principles

- Official WSET sources define grading authority.
- Non-official sources support pedagogy only.
- Distinguish correct knowledge from mark-worthy answers.
- Reward linkage, precision, relevance, and concision.
- Penalize vague, unsupported, or irrelevant content.
- Evaluate tasting evidence in the glass, not identity guessing.

## Agent Requirements

### Tutor Agent

The Tutor Agent teaches, explains, coaches, compresses, and trains. It may use official and non-official pedagogical sources, but it cannot override official evaluation standards.

### Examiner Agent

The Examiner Agent grades, scores, and evaluates. It must use official sources for calibration authority and clearly label practice rubrics when official mark schemes are unavailable.

### Orchestrator

The Orchestrator routes requests, stores results, maps weaknesses, triggers drills, and tracks learning progress.

## Required Capabilities

- Source reliability hierarchy
- Gold-standard calibration rules
- Error taxonomy
- Learning outcome mapping
- Cause-effect evaluation
- SAT tasting evaluation
- BICL consistency evaluation
- Internal consistency checks
- Benchmark wine architecture
- Student progress tracking
- Distinction coaching
- Concise-answer training
- Linkage detection
- Weak-area analytics
- Practice recommendation engine

## Success Metrics

- Student improves mark conversion rate.
- Student reduces repeated error taxonomy labels.
- Student produces shorter, clearer, more mark-worthy answers.
- Student supports tasting conclusions with SAT evidence.
- Examiner evaluations remain strict and repeatable.
- Tutor drills map directly to evaluated weaknesses.

## Risk Controls

- Do not invent official scoring systems.
- Do not let Tutor soften Examiner standards.
- Do not let non-official sources alter grading authority.
- Do not imply identity guessing is the center of SAT performance.
- Do not reward encyclopedic breadth without relevance.
- Do not use unauthorized acquisition of copyrighted or restricted material.
