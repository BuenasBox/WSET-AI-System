## 3. Explicación causa → efecto
**CAUSA:** A red wine with sufficient tannin, pigment, and structural components is aged in bottle over an extended period (years to decades)
**MECANISMO:** Tannin molecules and anthocyanin pigments polymerise (join together into larger molecules) and eventually precipitate out of solution; the wine's structure gradually softens as free tannins decrease
**EFECTO:** Sediment (dark, gritty deposit) forms in the bottle; the wine develops tertiary aromas (leather, earth, dried fruit, mushroom, tobacco); tannins become softer and more integrated
**FORMULACIÓN DE EXAMEN:** A structured red wine, through bottle ageing, sees tannins and pigments polymerise, forming sediment and developing tertiary aromas (leather, earth, dried fruit) over time.

*Relevancia SAT: Tertiary aromas and soft tannins signal bottle development on the SAT. A wine showing tertiary character has aged; readiness to drink should be assessed from tannin integration, fruit condition, and balance.*
