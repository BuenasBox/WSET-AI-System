## 3. Explicación causa → efecto
**CAUSA:** A solera system is established: a series of barrels (criaderas) arranged by age, each containing wine at different stages of maturation
**MECANISMO:** When wine is drawn from the oldest barrels (the solera level) for bottling, those barrels are partially refilled with younger wine from the next criadera level; this cascade continues up through all levels with the youngest wine topping the freshest criadera
**EFECTO:** Each bottling contains a blend of wines from multiple vintages; the consistent addition of older wine to younger wine and vice versa maintains a stable average age and style year after year
**FORMULACIÓN DE EXAMEN:** The solera's fractional blending process delivers consistency across vintages: wines from multiple age levels blend to maintain house character and style consistency regardless of harvest variation.

*Relevancia SAT: Solera-produced wines are typically assessed as non-vintage styles. Consistency across bottlings is the key feature. Complexity may be attributed partly to the blend of different aged components.*
